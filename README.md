## Project 04 - Healthcare Data Analysis - Benin

### Tools Used
- DB Browser for SQLite
- SQL (CREATE, INSERT, SELECT, WHERE,
  GROUP BY, ORDER BY, COUNT, AVG, JOIN, LIMIT)

### Database Structure
- Table: patients (10 records)
  id, name, city, disease, age, treatment_cost

### Key Queries & Findings

1. Most common disease:
   Malaria accounts for 40% of cases (4/10 patients)
   due to tropical climate and poor sanitation
   infrastructure in urban and rural areas.

2. Most expensive disease to treat:
   Diabetes has the highest average treatment cost
   (120,000 FCFA) due to limited medical equipment
   and lack of early detection resources in Benin.

3. Treatment cost by city:
   Parakou and Abomey show higher average costs
   than Cotonou, suggesting unequal distribution
   of healthcare resources across regions.

### Recommendation
The Beninese government should invest in healthcare
infrastructure and establish prevention committees
in underserved regions to reduce both disease
prevalence and treatment costs.
